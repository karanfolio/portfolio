Case Study

Launch Prototype

Staff scheduling mobile application.

Google Certification Project

About the Product

A personnel planning app for a restaurant owner to easily assign staff duties.

My Role and Responsibilities

User research, wireframing, low and high-fidelity prototyping and iterating on designs.

The Goal

The objective is to create an application to simplify his routine.

User research: summary

Quick Survey

I searched online and explored different applications that deliver the same services and compared them with the goals of the project I want to accomplish.

Pain Points
  • How to avoid any undesirable features that may slow down the user flow?
  • How to make it more useful and less of a learning application?
  • Staff scheduling applications may be very difficult to use.

Persona: Matt

User Story

As a Restaurant owner myself I want to Assign staff duties in way so that Every person in the staff can have a prior knowledge of the schedule.

Goals

Matt needs a simple online solution to manage staff-duties.

History

Matt did a research earlier on many online platforms, but didn’t find a solution

Problem

Matt schedule is too busy that he struggles to assign staff duties.

Problem Statement

Says:

Matt is a BBQ - restaurant owner who needs an easy way to manage his staff tasks because the assignment of daily staff tasks wastes a lot of his time.

Hypothesis Statement

Goes:

If We can present him with a mobile app that manages the functions of the staff, then he do not have to worry any longer to manage staff duties

Design Process

Paper Wireframes

Before going crazy over design, I personally love starting from a very basic design structure ie defining the base typography and layouts.

Takeaway

What I learned:

Throughout my Google Certification journey, I've learned to keep empathy and user flow in mind before designing anything.
